# Growth Loop

Work Wiki should help users improve, not only remember.

## Review Levels

1. Daily Capture: what happened and what evidence supports it.
2. Evidence Review: what is verified, partial, exploratory, or uncertain.
3. Pattern Mining: what repeats across weeks and months.
4. Growth Coaching: what experiments or training actions should change behavior.
5. Career Narrative: what long-term evidence can support performance reviews, promotion packets, portfolios, and resume material.

Version 0 supports levels 1 through 4.

## Scorecard

Track trends, not punitive scores:

- Deep work ratio
- Shipping rate
- Verification quality
- Learning density
- Context switching
- Collaboration load
- Energy signal

## Experiments

Each week should have at most one or two active experiments.

Each experiment should include:

- State: proposed / active / evaluating / adopted / dropped / promoted
- Hypothesis
- Duration
- Evidence to watch
- Expected behavior change
- Result
- Decision: adopt, revise, or drop

Lifecycle:

- `proposed` -> `active` or `dropped`
- `active` -> `evaluating` or `dropped`
- `evaluating` -> `adopted`, `dropped`, or `active`
- `adopted` -> `promoted`
- `promoted` means the practice has been captured in a durable index such as `indexes/workflows.md`

## Skill Graph

`indexes/skills.md` tracks skills through EvidenceRefs, related projects, practices, current level, gaps, and next training action.

## Recurring Problems

`indexes/recurring-problems.md` tracks repeated inefficiencies as improvement targets.

## Quality of Life

`growth/quality-of-life.md` captures work-adjacent signals such as overload, meeting pressure, repeated blockers, and lack of recovery time. It should stay evidence-based and should not become a private diary by default.
